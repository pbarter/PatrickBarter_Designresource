Why Design-Resource Was Made

To help designers both new and in the field have a website to find various links to resources like fonts and images and even online tutorials and helpful articles to expand your creativity.

Explore, View, Acquire, Design.

Objective

To provide various website links to further expand your graphic/web design knowledge and resources.

Fonts

Abobe Fonts

Got a Adobe Creative Could subscription? Well you already have access to thousands of free fonts! What is special about Adobe Fonts is that you can easily link a font to your website similar to how google fonts has it as well as add it to your library of fonts to use in your Adobe programs.

Visit Site

Google Fonts

Great font hosting site in which web designers and developers can link their font of choice to their HTML and CSS and have it show up for visitors of the website without having to download the font! Can also download fonts and use them on print work.

Visit Site

MyFonts

Professional looking fonts for various web or print related projects at a cost. Great if you have some spending money and want more well designed fonts.

Visit Site

Inspiration

Behance

Various media and design projects uploaded by designers both in the field and in school or practicing to show off their design work. Great website to find some inspiration. Also has frequent streams of designers using various Adobe software in design challenges.

Visit Site

Webdesign-inspiration

A website I discovered near the end of 2018, webdesign-inspiration.com is a website by one motivated web designer who links various websites that look appealing. Great way to explore what works, what doesn’t work and to even figure out how a certain designer coded a certain element in their site.

Visit Site

Dribble

Another inspiration/portfolio website like Behance in which designers can upload work and gain feedback. The only differenceis that Dibble has mostly smaller projects uploaded like a web page where Behance would have designers upload a whole website they designed.

Visit Site

Online Tutorials

Skillshare

Various free online tutorials for various subjects including both graphic and web design from industry professionals. Some videos are only viewable with a premium subscription for only $10 a month. A free month is offered when you sign up.

Visit Site

Lynda

A subscription based online tutorial website like Skillshare, what makes Lynda shine is not only the online tutorials but if you are in a post secondary school or own a library card, you may be eligable to use Lynda’s tutorials free!

Visit Site

Udemy

What makes Udemy stand out is that unlike Lynda and Skillshre in which you need to subscribe per month, you can just buy the tutorials you want on Udemy and have access to it forever. You also get an online certificate of completion upon completing the tutorials.

Visit Site

Websites

  • Lawsofux.com - Various user experience rules to take in to consideration when designing your websites.
  • Briefbox.me - Practice briefs for designers to create projects for pretend clients. Great way to build up your portfolio and gain some practice. Designers can also share their finished project and get critiques from mentors who are industry professionals or other users. Some breifs are free while others require a pro subcription.
  • Sharpen.Design - Another website in which you can get another design project idea but instead of a brief, these are more smaller projects like designing a contact page for a random business of choice or an icon that Twitter will use. Great for short quick projects to further enhance your design and software skills.
